About Me

I am one of those people who just can't help getting a kick out of life - even when it's a kick in the teeth.

Polly Adler


I was born and raised in Brooklyn, where I live today. It wasn't till the seventh grade that I was introduced to Computer Science, when my school introduced a new intiative to get middle schoolers into engineering. It started out with basic Python and Web Development, at which point I took it upon myself to learn more- and also introduce myself to Javascript. The following year, I was exposed to robotics, Scratch programming and circuitry- all of which I would grow to love and learn more about. My freshman year, I joined the robotics team- where I helped get us to the East Regional for the first time in our team's history. Sophomore year, I joined the Young Hackers; a group that aims to bring hackathons and programming to underprivelaged minorities. In addition, I co-founded Kinet-X- a STEM education company that aims to prepare the next generation to solve the problems ahead of them. For this project, I was selected as CE Week's Top 10 Under 20 Innovator' to look out for.

Here are some of my projects/acheivements.

Kinet-X

Started by me and two other sophomores, Kinet-X was born when we came to the realization that STEM was simply too hard to get into. School's around the country aren't certified to teach CS, and programs around the city are overpriced and lack the teaching methods to be effective. We set out to change that. Learn more at kinet-x.org

Team 479 Stuy Fusion

One of Stuyvesant's FTC robotics teams, I'm proud to be the team's Head of Engineering. My responsibilities include designing the robot at the beginning of the year, when we find the game out, teaching new members the basics of engineering and helping build the robot during the season. Along with building and programming robots, the team also does a lot of community outreach, to spread STEM. Learn more at our website stuyfusion.github.io

Young Hackers

The Young Hackers are a group of high school students across New York City that aim to bring STEM to minorities and those that might not have access to it. I joined in April of 2016, after attending their super amazing event Hip Hop Hacks, and have been a dedicated member ever since. I currently serve as their Head of Outreach as well as Website Manager. Find out more about the Young Hackers at younghackers.us
